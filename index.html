lesson #3-Use "square breathing" to get past sudden bouts of anxiety,then move on step-by-step.

Dr Smith offers a technique:
Find a Comfortable Position: Start by sitting or lying down in a comfortable position.
Close your eyes if it helps you relax.
Focus on Your Breath: Take a few deep breaths to settle into the practice.
Pay attention to the sensation of your breath entering and leaving your body.
Visualize a Square: Imagine a square in front of you.
As you inhale, trace one side of the square in your mind. As you exhale, trace the next side of the square.
Controlled Breathing: Breathe in slowly and deeply through your nose for a count of four as you trace one side of the square.
Hold your breath for a count of four as you trace the next side.
Exhale slowly and completely through your mouth for a count of four as you trace the third side.
Hold your breath again for a count of four as you complete the square.
Repeat: Continue this pattern of breathing, focusing on the square visualization and maintaining a steady rhythm.
Aim to make each breath smooth and even, without straining.
Practice Regularly: Square breathing is most effective when practiced regularly, especially during times of stress or anxiety.
You can use this technique anytime and anywhere you feel the need to calm your mind and body.
